# Brand Bible — MVP Fictional Brands

> **Legal posture:** All brands at MVP are fictional. Original names, original logos, original colorways. No replicated real-world brand marks, trade dress, sole patterns, box logos, or graphic prints. Reviewed by counsel before public launch. Real-brand licensing is a Phase 2+ conversation.

This file is the master list. Anything not in this file does not exist in-game.

## House brand

### Hubba

The in-house label. Always in stock, never numbered, unlimited supply. The "starter wardrobe." Hubba items can be reskinned for seasons.

- Categories: T-shirts, hoodies, beanies, blank decks
- Visual identity: clean, blocky logotype; brand color tbd (placeholder: deep red `#a51c1c`)
- Role: gives every player a baseline closet without a single drop

## Working fictional roster (5 brands at MVP)

These are working names — counsel will clear them before launch. The aesthetic positioning is what matters; the names can swap.

### 1. **Thrashr** — magazine-culture / DIY

- **Vibe:** zine aesthetic, rough type, "skate or die" posture
- **Categories:** T-shirts, hoodies, magazine props for closet decor (deco only, not wearable)
- **Edition pattern:** seasonal common (5,000–10,000), no numbered grails
- **Inspired by:** the *category* of skate magazine merch — not Thrasher specifically

### 2. **Polestar Decks** — heritage deck brand

- **Vibe:** Powell/Santa Cruz-era graphic decks, illustrated, narrative
- **Categories:** Decks (the main category), grip tape patterns, sticker packs
- **Edition pattern:** numbered limited (250–500) for signature pro models; unlimited for blanks
- **Role:** the "Tony Hawk deck collector" experience — players hunt low serials
- **Inspired by:** Powell-Peralta, Santa Cruz, Vision-era graphic decks

### 3. **Suprmthing** — drop-culture streetwear

- **Vibe:** box-logo hype, scarcity-driven, weekly drops
- **Categories:** Hoodies, tees, caps, beanies
- **Edition pattern:** weekly drops, 1,000–2,000 per drop, no restocks
- **Role:** the "is the drop today?" engagement hook
- **Inspired by:** the *mechanic* of Supreme drops — not its actual graphics

### 4. **Vanz** *(working name — likely renaming)* — sturdy vulc shoes

- **Vibe:** durable vulcanized sole shoes, classic silhouettes (low, hi-top, slip-on)
- **Categories:** Shoes only
- **Edition pattern:** standard colorways unlimited, seasonal collabs numbered (500–1,000)
- **Note:** likely needs rename — "Vanz" is too close to Vans for counsel comfort. Backup: *Trakz*, *Sole Co.*, *Vulkn*.
- **Inspired by:** the *category* of vulc skate shoes

### 5. **Driftway** — technical performance shoes

- **Vibe:** chunky, cushioned, signature pro models with named lines
- **Categories:** Shoes, technical apparel (cargo pants, performance tees)
- **Edition pattern:** signature pro shoe models numbered 1,000–2,500; team riders rotate seasonally
- **Role:** the "Koston / Eric Koston / Marc Johnson signature pro model" archetype
- **Inspired by:** the *category* of ES, Etnies, Emerica, Lakai pro models

## What "inspired by" means in practice

- We borrow the **category, vibe, and drop mechanic**.
- We do **not** borrow logos, trade dress, specific colorways, graphics, fonts, sole patterns, or marketing taglines.
- Each brand has its own original logo, brand colors, fictional founder story, and visual language documented in `brand-bible-extended.md` (TBD).

## Real-brand path (Phase 2+, post-PMF)

Once we have traction, the licensing playbook:

1. **Year 1–2: Mid-tier indie skate brands** — approach Polar, Hockey, Quasi, Welcome, Hopps, or similar. Smaller legal teams, hungry for reach, co-marketing-friendly. Target deals: $20–100K GMR per brand, 7–10% royalty.
2. **Year 2+: Hero brand collab** — Thrasher, Birdhouse, or a Tony Hawk signature deck collab. Marketing moment. Single brand, single drop.
3. **Year 3+: Major apparel portfolio** — Nike SB, Vans, Emerica, Lakai. Heavy negotiation, $100–500K GMR per brand, 10–15% royalty. Needs a real licensing manager on staff.

## Brand asset checklist (before any item ships)

For each brand:
- [ ] Original wordmark (SVG, 3 weights)
- [ ] Original brand mark / icon (SVG)
- [ ] Brand color palette (3–5 colors, hex)
- [ ] Brand voice + 200-word fictional history
- [ ] At least 5 item designs in the brand's hero category
- [ ] Counsel sign-off on name + mark before public listing

## Naming legal-safety notes

- Do a USPTO TESS search + EU TMview + WIPO Global Brand Database search on every name before launch.
- Avoid names that are 1-letter swaps of real brands (e.g. `Suprmthing` is borderline — flag for counsel).
- Avoid names that combine real-brand fragments (e.g. `Nike SB` style fragment + skate term).
- Avoid logo silhouettes that read like real marks at a glance — counsel reviews the visual, not just the name.
